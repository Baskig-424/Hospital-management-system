What is a Lung Transplant?

A lung transplant is a surgical procedure in which a diseased or damaged lung is replaced with a healthy lung from a donor. This treatment is usually recommended for patients suffering from severe lung diseases when other treatments are no longer effective.

Common Diseases Requiring Lung Transplant

  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
  • Pulmonary Fibrosis
  • Cystic Fibrosis
  • Pulmonary Hypertension

Symptoms of Severe Lung Disease

  • Difficulty in breathing
  • Frequent lung infections
  • Low oxygen levels
  • Severe fatigue

Benefits of Lung Transplant

  • Improved breathing
  • Better quality of life
  • Increased life expectancy
  • Better physical activity

Risks and Complications

  • Infection
  • Organ rejection
  • Bleeding
  • Side effects of medicines

Lung Transplant Procedure

  1. Patient evaluation and medical tests.
  2. Finding a suitable donor lung.
  3. Performing transplant surgery under anesthesia.
  4. Replacing damaged lung with healthy donor lung.
  5. Post-surgery monitoring in ICU.
  6. Long-term recovery and medications.

Recovery After Surgery

After the transplant, patients must take special medications to prevent organ rejection. Regular medical checkups, healthy lifestyle, and proper rehabilitation programs help patients recover faster and live healthier lives.
